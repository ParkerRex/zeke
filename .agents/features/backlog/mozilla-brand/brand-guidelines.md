# Brand Guidelines for Mozilla

## Brand Personality

Mozilla's visual identity embodies a bold, open, and radical approach to the internet. The design system reflects their commitment to transparency, global perspective, and user empowerment. The brand features vibrant neon colors, geometric typography, and generous spacing that creates a modern, accessible, and trustworthy digital experience.

## Design Principles

1. **Flexible & Adaptive** - Components accommodate unpredictability in content, window sizes, and layouts. The design prioritizes flexibility over precision.

2. **Accessible & Inclusive** - The design system welcomes the widest possible audience, considering diverse abilities, devices, browsers, and network conditions.

3. **Global & Localizable** - Designs translate well across nations, cultures, and languages with thoughtful consideration for international audiences.

4. **Bold & Optimistic** - Uses vibrant colors and generous spacing to create an optimistic, forward-thinking aesthetic that disrupts the status quo.

5. **Transparent & Open** - Clear visual hierarchy and open spacing reflect Mozilla's commitment to transparency and open-source values.

## Visual Hierarchy

Mozilla achieves visual hierarchy through:

- **Typography Scale**: Clear distinction between Mozilla Headline (display text > 24px) and Mozilla Text (body text)
- **Color Contrast**: High contrast between vibrant brand colors and neutral backgrounds
- **Spatial Rhythm**: Consistent spacing tokens that create predictable visual rhythm
- **Component Hierarchy**: Primary actions use bold colors, secondary actions use outlined styles

## Color Philosophy

The Mozilla color system features:

- **Neon Accents**: Vibrant cyan, green, yellow, and red for emphasis and energy
- **Deep Purples**: Dark purple as a grounding brand color
- **Extensive Grays**: Comprehensive neutral palette for UI flexibility
- **Semantic Colors**: Clear success, warning, and error states

## Typography Strategy

- **Mozilla Headline**: Custom uniwidth typeface for headlines and display text
- **Mozilla Text**: Readable body font maintaining consistent spacing across weights
- **Metropolis**: Firefox-specific geometric sans-serif for product experiences
- **Inter**: Fallback and body text font for optimal readability

## Spacing System

Two-tier spacing approach:

- **Component Spacing** (`$spacing-*`): 4px base unit for tight internal spacing
- **Layout Spacing** (`$layout-*`): 16px base unit for generous section spacing

## Component Patterns

- **Buttons**: Clear primary/secondary hierarchy with consistent padding and border radius
- **Cards**: Subtle shadows and generous padding for content separation
- **Forms**: Accessible inputs with clear focus states and error handling
- **Navigation**: Flexible patterns that adapt to mobile and desktop contexts
